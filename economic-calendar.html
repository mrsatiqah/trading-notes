Economic Calendar: Why Every Trader Should Check It

Master the scheduled earthquakes that can instantly make your technical analysis irrelevant, or create massive opportunities for those who know how to navigate them.

Imagine you've found the perfect trade. The trend is clear, your technical analysis looks solid, your stop loss is in place. Everything seems to be going according to plan.

Then, without warning, the market suddenly makes a huge move against you. Within seconds, your stop loss is hit. You look at the chart wondering what just happened. Later, you realise one important thing, a major economic news release had just been announced.

This happens to many traders, especially beginners who forget to check the economic calendar before placing a trade.

The Market Doesn't Only Move Because of Charts

Technical analysis is important, but sometimes one piece of economic news is enough to completely change market sentiment. Price can move hundreds of points within minutes. No chart pattern can predict exactly how the market will react to unexpected news, that's why every trader should make checking the economic calendar part of their daily routine.

What Is an Economic Calendar?

An economic calendar is simply a schedule of upcoming economic events and announcements that may affect the financial markets. These include employment reports, inflation data, interest rate decisions, GDP reports, consumer spending data, and central bank speeches. These events can create large price movements in currencies, stocks, commodities, bonds, and even cryptocurrencies.

Why It Matters

Technical analysis looks at what has already happened. Economic news introduces brand-new information into the market, sometimes that information confirms the current trend, sometimes it changes everything. That's why experienced traders always know what major events are scheduled before they start trading.

Important Economic Events

Some news releases have a much bigger impact than others.

Non-Farm Payrolls (NFP)
First Friday of Every Month
Measures employment growth in the United States. It is one of the most closely watched economic reports in the world.
Impact: Strong or weak numbers can move the US Dollar, stock markets, and bond markets very quickly.
Federal Reserve Meetings
Interest Rate Decisions
Can affect almost every financial market. Sometimes the interest rate itself isn't the biggest surprise.
Key Factor: Traders pay close attention to what the Federal Reserve says about the future.
Consumer Price Index (CPI)
Measures Inflation
Since inflation influences interest rate decisions, this report often creates significant market volatility.
Gross Domestic Product (GDP)
Overall Economic Growth
Helps investors understand whether the economy is expanding or slowing down.
Retail Sales
Consumer Spending Data
Consumer spending plays a major role in economic growth. Strong retail sales usually suggest consumers are confident and spending more.
Expectations Matter More Than the Numbers

Markets don't always react to whether the news is "good" or "bad." Instead, they react to whether the actual result is better or worse than what traders expected. For example, if the market expects 200,000 new jobs but the report shows 150,000, the market may fall even though jobs were still added. Expectations often move markets more than the actual numbers.

How Markets Usually React

Most major news events follow a similar pattern:

1
Immediate Reaction
First Few Minutes
Algorithms and high-frequency traders react almost instantly, large candles and sudden price spikes are common.
2
Market Interpretation
After the Initial Move
Traders begin analysing the details, sometimes the first move continues, other times the market completely reverses.
3
Longer-Term Trend
Next Few Hours to a Day
The market begins deciding what the news really means, this often creates the longer-term direction.
Example Reaction:
Expected: 200,000 new jobs
Actual: 150,000 new jobs
Market Reaction: May fall, even though jobs were still added

Expectations often move markets more than the actual numbers.

Different Ways to Trade Around News

There isn't one correct approach, choose the one that matches your experience and risk tolerance.

Trade Before the News
For Traders with a Strong View
  • Enter positions before the announcement based on a strong market view
  • Carries higher risk because unexpected results can create violent price swings
  • Consider reducing your position size if you choose this approach
Wait for the News
Generally Safer for Newer Traders
  • Let the market react first instead of guessing the result
  • Once the direction becomes clearer, look for trading opportunities
  • A generally safer approach for newer traders
Stay Out Completely
Protecting Your Capital
  • Nothing wrong with avoiding major news events altogether
  • Many technical traders wait until volatility settles before looking for new setups
  • Sometimes protecting your capital is the best trade you can make

Managing Risk During News Events

Market conditions change during major announcements, your risk management should change too:

  • Reduce Position Size: Large price swings mean larger risk, many traders reduce their position size before important news releases
  • Give Trades More Room: Volatility can trigger tight stop losses easily, if you widen your stop loss, remember to reduce your position size so overall risk stays the same
  • Expect Wider Spreads: During major news events, spreads often become much larger, increasing your trading costs and affecting your entries and exits
  • Prepare for Slippage: In fast-moving markets, your trade may not be filled at the exact price you expected, this is completely normal during periods of high volatility
Don't Forget the Technology

News events create huge trading activity, this is often when trading platforms experience delays or temporary issues. Before trading major news, make sure your internet connection is stable, your trading platform is working properly, and you know what to do if technical problems occur. Having a backup plan can save you a lot of stress.

Common Mistakes

Trying to Predict Every News Event
Nobody can consistently predict how markets will react.
Fix: Focus on managing your risk instead of trying to be right every time.
Ignoring the Bigger Picture
One news event doesn't always change the overall trend.
Fix: Always consider the broader market context before making decisions.
Poor Timing
Some traders enter too early, others chase the move after it's already happened.
Fix: Patience often produces better opportunities.
Using Normal Position Sizes
Large market moves require different risk management.
Fix: Don't trade major news the same way you trade during normal market conditions.

Final Thoughts

Economic news is part of trading. You don't need to fear it, but you should always respect it.

Before placing any trade, spend a minute checking the economic calendar. Knowing when important announcements are coming can help you avoid unnecessary losses and better prepare for market volatility.

The goal isn't to predict every news release. The goal is to manage your risk and avoid being caught by surprise.

Key Takeaways

  • The economic calendar shows scheduled events that can significantly move the markets
  • Major events include NFP, Federal Reserve meetings, CPI, GDP, and Retail Sales
  • Markets react to whether results are better or worse than expectations, not just whether they're good or bad
  • Most news events follow three stages: the initial reaction, market interpretation, and longer-term trend
  • You can trade before the news, after confirmation, or simply stay out until volatility settles
  • Reduce position sizes and adjust your risk management during major news events
  • Always check the economic calendar before trading to avoid unnecessary surprises
